1. Dock Queue Scenario
Multiple forklifts arrive at a dock door faster than the loading process can handle. Vehicles begin to line up, forming a queue that exceeds normal operating thresholds. The queue persists over time, with limited forward movement, indicating a mismatch between arrival rate and loading throughput. This scenario tests the system’s ability to detect sustained queuing, measure peak queue size, and identify doors becoming bottlenecks.

2. Loading Pause Scenario
A dock door is open and assigned for loading, but material handling activity suddenly stops. Forklifts are either absent or waiting without active loading taking place. The door remains open for an extended period while no loading progress occurs, suggesting operational disruption such as labor gaps, coordination issues, or delays in material availability. This scenario validates detection of idle dock time during active door states.

3. Aisle Congestion Scenario
Several forklifts operate within the same aisle, causing increased vehicle density and reduced movement speed. Traffic flow becomes restricted as vehicles slow down or stop intermittently due to limited space and interactions with each other. The congestion persists long enough to indicate a developing bottleneck rather than a temporary slowdown. This scenario evaluates detection of high-density zones and reduced mobility patterns.

4. Area Build-Up Scenario
Forklifts begin accumulating in a specific operational zone such as staging, packing, or transfer areas. Vehicles enter the area faster than they can exit, leading to crowding and increased dwell time. Movement becomes inefficient, and flow slows across the area, indicating a systemic bottleneck rather than isolated congestion. This scenario tests the system’s ability to identify build-ups and prolonged flow imbalance.

5. Vehicle Idle Scenario
A forklift remains stationary in a non-designated idle zone for an extended duration without performing any task. The vehicle is not actively loading, unloading, or moving, suggesting potential inefficiencies such as operator inactivity, task delays, or system misallocation. This scenario is used to validate detection of abnormal idle behavior and prolonged inactivity at the vehicle level.

6. Safety Proximity (Near-Miss) Scenario
Two or more forklifts come into dangerously close proximity while moving within the warehouse, such as at intersections, aisles, or near dock doors. Their paths intersect or overlap in a way that creates a high-risk situation, requiring sudden braking or evasive action. No collision occurs, but the interaction represents a near-miss event. This scenario evaluates the system’s ability to detect unsafe distances and high-risk interactions in real time.
